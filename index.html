ABOUT US

CalibraML is a Cape Town-based industrial technology firm specializing in advanced sensor calibration solutions. Developed in partnership with the University of Cape Town, we deliver cutting-edge instrumentation models that optimize industrial processes through enhanced data reliability.

Our flagship technology, the Industrial Blind Calibration System (IBCS), represents years of collaborative research between academic and industry experts. This passive supervisory system is designed for IEC 62264 Level 2 compliance, extending sensor life and reducing measurement errors without costly sensor arrays.

We're committed to solving real industrial challenges through data-driven innovation, helping partners optimize their processes while minimizing operational costs and downtime.

  • IEC 62264 Level 2 compliant solution.
  • Zero intrusion supervisory architecture.
  • Machine learning and numerical methods expertise.
  • Strategic University of Cape Town partnership.

Meet Our Team

Led by industry and academic experts committed to solving complex industrial challenges

Professor Amit Mishra

Director and Chief Innovator

A prolific innovator with multiple patents in topics ranging from radar to AI to ASIC-design, Amit has worked in four continents on a wide range of co-innovation challenges and research projects. He has published more than 180 papers in peer-reviewed journals and conferences and has supervised 11 PhD students to completion so far. Due to our international and interdisciplinary work, Amit also works as an Adjunct Professor in the University of Cape Town, University College London, University of Sunshine Coast (Australia), University West Sweden and AGH University Poland.

James Hepworth

Director and Engineer

James is a experienced engineer with a diverse background in the fields of embedded systems, sensor optimisation and geo-spatial data analysis. Thomas Roomes

Engineer

Thomas is a candidate MSc. (Eng) in Mechatronics Engineering at the University of Cape Town, having completed a BSc. (Eng) in Mechanical and Mechatronic Engineering in 2024. The focus of his current research concerns the implementation of algorithms for then blind calibration of sensor outputs. He has gained experience in embedded systems development, PCB design, calibration, and mechanical manufacturing through projects in both South Africa and Germany.
